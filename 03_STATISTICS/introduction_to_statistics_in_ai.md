# Chapter 03: Statistics in Artificial Intelligence

This chapter begins where the Mathematics chapter breaks.

In Chapter 02, we built AI around:

- states
- actions
- search
- logic
- rules
- planning

That world was clean.
It worked best when:

- facts were known
- rules were stable
- actions behaved as expected

But real life is not like that.

- Sensors are noisy
- Knowledge is incomplete
- Outcomes are uncertain
- The same action can lead to different results

So AI had to take a major step:

> it had to stop reasoning only about what is true, and start reasoning about what is likely

That is where statistics enters.

This chapter is not a side topic.
It is the next necessary shift in the lineage.

Probability gave AI a language for uncertainty.
Probabilistic models gave AI a way to represent uncertain worlds.
Decision theory gave AI a way to act when outcomes are not guaranteed.

So the chapter moves in three connected stages:

1. **Probability Replaced Pure Logic**
   We move from hard true/false statements to belief under uncertainty.

2. **Probabilistic Models**
   We learn how uncertain variables and relationships can be represented mathematically.

3. **Decision Making Under Uncertainty**
   We combine belief with action, reward, and long-term choice.

This chapter matters because modern AI did not grow from logic alone.
It also grew from the ability to model uncertainty and act despite it.

And once AI learns to do that well, the next question becomes unavoidable:

> can the system learn these patterns from data instead of depending only on hand-built probability structure?

That question leads into the next chapter:

**Machine Learning**.
